Research

My research develops data- and learning-based methods for analyzing and controlling dynamical systems, with an emphasis on rigorous guarantees, efficient algorithms, and applications where reliable models or complete measurements are difficult to obtain.

Data-based representations and output-feedback control

How can we represent, analyze, and control a dynamical system using only short, noisy, and potentially irregularly measured input–output data?

Why it matters. A mathematical model is not always available, and collecting long, perfectly sampled datasets may be expensive or simply impossible. Working directly with measured data can make analysis and controller design possible when conventional model-based methods are difficult to use.

My work. I develop data-based representations that capture the behavior of linear systems from limited input–output measurements. My contributions also address missing samples, the design of sufficiently informative experiments, and output-feedback control of multivariable systems directly from data.

Representative work

  • System representations from irregularly measured data
  • Data-driven output-feedback control of linear MIMO systems
  • Design of persistently exciting inputs

Data-driven control of nonlinear systems

How can we analyze and control a nonlinear system directly from data when its equations are unknown or too complicated to model accurately?

Why it matters. Nonlinear behavior appears in many physical and engineered systems, but most data-driven methods are easier to develop for linear systems. Extending reliable methods to nonlinear dynamics allows us to address a much broader range of practical control problems.

My work. I developed data-based representations and controllers for important classes of nonlinear systems, including flat and feedback-linearizable systems. This work covers simulation, output matching, predictive control, and stabilization methods that account for noisy data and help estimate the region in which the controller can stabilize the system.

Representative work

  • Data-based control of feedback-linearizable systems
  • Data-driven nonlinear predictive control
  • Nonlinear stabilization via descriptor embedding

Predictive and learning-based control

How can a controller use previously collected data to predict future behavior and learn good control actions without requiring large datasets or expensive online computation?

Why it matters. Predictive control and reinforcement learning can choose control actions by looking ahead or learning from experience, but many methods require large amounts of data and computation. Reducing these requirements is important when experiments are costly or control decisions must be made in real time.

My work. I developed robust data-driven predictive control methods that work with short, noisy, and potentially irregular data while providing closed-loop guarantees. I also worked on efficient off-policy Q-learning methods that learn optimal output-feedback policies from a single offline input–output experiment, without a mathematical model or state measurements.

Representative work

  • Robust and efficient data-driven predictive control
  • Efficient data-based Q-learning for output-feedback control
  • Off-policy Q-learning for discrete-time LQR

Biomedical control applications

How can feedback control help determine the right fluid infusion rate for an individual patient?

Why it matters. Patients can respond differently to the same treatment, and their condition may change over time. A closed-loop system can use physiological measurements such as blood pressure to adjust the infusion rate according to the patient's response.

My work. I developed and evaluated closed-loop algorithms for automated fluid resuscitation in simulation. My recent work explores a data-driven predictive controller that personalizes fluid delivery without first identifying a patient-specific model, evaluated on 100 virtual patients.

Representative work

  • Data-driven patient-specific fluid resuscitation
  • Closed-loop hemorrhage resuscitation using blood-pressure feedback
